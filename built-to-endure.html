Built to Endure

How do we endure in life?

Dec. 1st The Solid Rock Atlanta

Ice Breaker: How would you define "endurance"?

Opening Thought: In the Word of GOD, we find a great emphasis placed upon the importance of endurance. The first verse of Hebrews 12 instructs us to run “with endurance” the race that has been set before us. Moreover, Hebrews 10:36 reveals that we have need of endurance in order to receive the promises of GOD.

So, what is endurance? From a careful study of Scripture, one could define endurance as: faithfulness where GOD has you, as long as He has you there, even when the costs appear to outweigh the benefits and the losses appear to outweigh the gains. In reality, when you are in the center of GOD’S Will, it is impossible for you to lose, but often our carnal understanding distorts our perception, causing us to believe that the losses outweigh the gains.

In such times, endurance comes from the determination to trust GOD regardless of what you see in the natural. It comes from the decision to hold on to the WORD even when your natural circumstances appear to be contrary to the WORD. How do we endure in life? We find a key to endurance when we read Hebrews 12:2-3. The writer of Hebrews first tells us in Hebrews 12:2, that JESUS endured the Cross because of the Joy that was set before Him. Then, in the following verse we read, “For consider Him who endured such hostility from sinners against Himself, lest you become weary and discouraged in your souls” [NKJV]. So, the writer of Hebrews begins by telling us how JESUS endured – for the joy that was set before Him – then he tells us to consider the way JESUS endured so that we don’t become discouraged in our own souls.

When you consider the way JESUS endured the Cross, you will be empowered to endure anything in this life. To endure in this life, you must choose to see the unseen. The Apostle Paul wrote that he did not lose heart because he chose not to look at the temporary things that are seen, but rather the eternal things that are unseen [II Cor 4:16-18]. In the same way, the Word teaches that Moses endured as seeing Him who is invisible [Heb 11:27].

GOD created your heart with the capacity to see the unseen, to look above the cloud barrier. While you stand, waiting for the storm to pass, chose to see above the cloud barrier. Don’t limit yourself to what can be seen beneath the clouds, because everything beneath the clouds is temporary and subject to change. What you choose to see will determine your ability to endure.

Final thought: True endurance in not the resolve to suffer through affliction, but rather the determination to stand through affliction. JESUS suffered so that you could stand! Take your stand today, because you were built to endure!

Opening Scripture

“ For you have need of endurance, so that after you have done the will of God, you may receive the promise:”—Hebrews 10:36[NKJV]

Discuss

  1. Read Hebrews 12:1-3. Vs 1: What does it mean to run our race “with endurance”?
  2. [Continue from Heb 12:1-3] Vs 2: What enabled JESUS to endure the Cross?
  3. Read II Corinthians 4:16-18. According to this passage, what is the key to living without losing heart? What does it mean to see the unseen?
  4. Read Galatians 6:9. Why is it essential that we never lose heart?
  5. Read Habakkuk 3:17-19. What does this passage teach us about endurance?
  6. Discuss the following statement as a group: “True endurance in not the resolve to suffer through affliction, but rather the determination to stand through affliction. JESUS suffered so that you could stand!“
